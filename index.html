Instagram Reels Playbook

A template-based system for consistent, high-engagement content
Prepared for Robyn Gabrielson  ·  April 2026

The bottleneck isn't filming or ideas. It's that every Reel requires too many decisions. This playbook collapses those decisions to near-zero through rigid templates, ranked by what the data shows actually works at your audience size. Pick a template, follow the recipe, post.

Reel Templates

Tier 1 — High Engagement — Post 2-3x/week
#1 — "Would You Pick This Home?"
Engagement Bait
~8 min edit
Your own "which one would you choose?" Reel got 6x more comments than educational content. Questions in the hook are the strongest driver for algorithm reach at smaller account sizes.

Hook (first 1-3 seconds)

  • This home is $1.1M in Scottsdale. Would you change anything?
  • Rate this kitchen 1-10
  • Would you keep this or renovate?
  • Dream home or pass?
  • Pick your favorite room

Shot List (film in this order)

  1. Wide entry or establishing shot — hold 8-10 sec, trim to 4
  2. Kitchen or main living area — hold 8-10 sec
  3. Primary suite or bathroom — hold 8-10 sec
  4. Best unique feature: pool, view, fireplace — hold 8-10 sec
  5. Hook shot: the most striking single frame — film last, use as opener

Script / Text Overlay

0:00"This $___K home in ______"
0:04Price + key specs (beds / baths / sqft)
End"Tell me in the comments"
Audio
Trending audio from CapCut. No voiceover.
Tone
Let the house speak. Minimal text. The question does the work.

Caption Template

This $___K home in ______ has me questioning everything. Would you change anything? Tell me below.

#ScottsdaleRealEstate #GilbertAZ #DreamHome #AZHomes #HouseHunting
#2 — "Walk With Me"
Neighborhood / Lifestyle
~12 min edit
Your neighborhood walk Reel outperformed all other content. This is where personality shows. At your follower count, being someone viewers want to follow matters more than showcasing a property.

Hook (first 1-3 seconds)

  • Come walk this Scottsdale neighborhood with me
  • POV: house hunting in Gilbert
  • I found something cool on my walk today
  • This is the neighborhood everyone asks me about

Shot List (film in this order)

  1. Walking shot, camera following from behind or beside — 10 sec
  2. Interesting home exterior or architectural detail — 8 sec
  3. Local spot: coffee shop, park entrance, street scene — 8 sec
  4. Second home or neighborhood feature — 8 sec
  5. Sunset, mountain view, or wide neighborhood shot — 8 sec

Script

Casual narration while walking. "I love this street because..." / "This is the kind of neighborhood where..." Keep it conversational, like talking to a friend on FaceTime.
Audio
Soft music underneath voice
Tone
Natural, curious, enthusiastic without being performative. This is the "I want to work with her" content.

Caption Template

Took a walk through ______ and fell in love with the vibe. Which house caught your eye?

#WalkWithMe #AZRealEstate #ScottsdaleLife #GilbertAZ
#3 — "Three Things I Love"
Quick Hit
~8 min edit
Short, punchy, lowest effort. 15-20 second Reels get the highest completion rates, and the algorithm rewards completion over watch time at smaller account sizes. This is the fastest template to film and edit.

Hook (first 1-3 seconds)

  • Three things I love about this $950K home
  • Three features you won't believe at this price
  • Three reasons this home won't last

Shot List (film in this order)

  1. Feature #1 — close-up detail shot — hold 8 sec, trim to 4
  2. Feature #2 — close-up detail shot — hold 8 sec, trim to 4
  3. Feature #3 — close-up detail shot — hold 8 sec, trim to 4
  4. Hook shot: holding up three fingers or pointing at camera — film last

Script

One sentence per thing. Observational, not salesy. "This kitchen island could seat eight." / "The primary closet has better lighting than most bathrooms." / "And this backyard at this price point?"
Audio
Trending audio, fast cuts aligned to beats
Tone
Excited but specific. Name what makes it good. No generic superlatives.

Caption Template

Three things that made my jaw drop at this $___K listing in ______. Which one's your favorite?

#ThreeThingsILove #AZRealEstate #ScottsdaleHomes #GilbertHomes #LuxuryLiving
Tier 2 — Brand Building — Post 1-2x/week, rotate
#4 — "Day in the Life"
Behind the Scenes
~12 min edit
Builds the "know, like, trust" factor that converts followers to clients over 6 months. Harder to batch-film, so it's supplemental. Film passively throughout the day; don't stage it.

Hook Options

  • What showing a $1M home actually looks like
  • The part of real estate no one talks about
  • A day in my life as a Scottsdale agent

Shot List

  1. Morning routine or getting ready — 5 sec
  2. Driving to showing or walking up to a home — 5 sec
  3. Inside the home, one wow moment — 5 sec
  4. Real reaction or candid moment — 5 sec
  5. End of day or wrap-up — 5 sec
Script
Casual voiceover added after, or real-time narration. Show the unglamorous parts too.
Audio
Light background music under voice
#5 — "The Reveal"
Cinematic Tour
~10 min edit
Polished silent walkthroughs consistently get the lowest engagement even at 200K+ followers. Beautiful but doesn't drive comments. Use only for the absolute best homes or portfolio pieces. This is the template worth outsourcing for extra polish.

Hook Options

  • Wait for it...
  • This $1.2M Scottsdale home surprised me

Shot List

  1. Extreme close-up detail (faucet, countertop, fireplace) — hold 10 sec, trim to 3
  2. Slow pull-back to reveal the room — hold 10 sec, trim to 5
  3. Walking transition to next best room — hold 10 sec, trim to 4
  4. Wide shot of best feature (pool, view, exterior) — hold 10 sec, trim to 5
Script
No talking. Text overlays only, timed to transitions. 3-4 short lines max.
Audio
Moody, slow-build instrumental. Cut transitions to beat drops.
#6 — "Market Take"
Hot Take / Opinion
~10 min edit
Educational content without opinion gets low engagement. Tie your take to a specific home or neighborhood. One point, strong stance, 15-20 seconds. Opinionated content builds authority.

Hook Options

  • Unpopular opinion about Scottsdale real estate
  • The biggest mistake I see buyers making at $1M
  • Nobody talks about this in Gilbert real estate

Shot List

  1. Talking to camera, inside a listing as backdrop — 15-20 sec total
  2. Optional: B-roll of the home over voiceover
Script
One point. Strong stance. "Here's what I think..." End with "Agree or disagree?" or "Am I wrong?"
Audio
Optional, low background music

Batch Filming Protocol

Walk into a vacant home once, walk out with 2-3 Reels of raw footage in under 30 minutes.

Before Arriving

Pick 2-3 recipe cards that fit this home. Pull up the shot lists on your phone. Not every template fits every home.

At the Property

Scout
5 minutes

Walk through without filming. Identify the 3-4 strongest features and any conversation-starter elements (bold paint, unusual layout, amazing view).

Film in Template Order
15-20 minutes

Shoot all clips for Template A, then all clips for Template B, then C. Do NOT interleave. Each template's clips will be grouped on your camera roll in editing order.

Story Content
3 minutes

Grab 3-4 casual clips for Stories. No gimbal needed. Point at something interesting, say one sentence, post raw. Use poll or question stickers.

Filming Rules

  • Hold each shot 8-10 seconds even though the final clip will be 3-5 seconds. Gives editing room.
  • Film the hook shot last. You'll know the best angle after walking the house.
  • If talking, 2 takes max. First take is usually the most natural. Don't chase perfection.
  • Gimbal on for walking shots. Handheld is fine for static detail shots.

After Leaving

  • Clips are already grouped by template on camera roll
  • Edit within 24 hours while the home is fresh
  • Stay 1-2 Reels ahead to remove "I need to post today" pressure

Editing Workflow (CapCut)

One-Time Setup

Do this once. It eliminates the micro-decisions that make editing feel slow.

  • Create a CapCut project template for each recipe card you use
  • Pre-set 9:16 aspect ratio in each template
  • Save your go-to font, size, color, and position for text overlays
  • Save 3-4 music tracks per template mood (moody, upbeat, trending)
  • Set auto-captions to your preferred style

Per-Reel Edit Sequence

1

Import + Assemble

5 minutes

Import clips (already in order from filming). Drop on timeline in sequence. Trim each clip to the target duration from the recipe card.

2

Layer

5 minutes

Add text overlays (copy from recipe card). Drop in saved music track. Auto-generate captions, fix any misreadings. Align cuts to beats if applicable.

3

Export + Post

3 minutes

Export at highest quality. Post or save to camera roll. Copy/paste caption template, fill in the blanks. Add to Story with engagement sticker (poll, question, slider).

Posting Checklist

  • Pick the most visually striking frame as cover image
  • Write caption from template, fill in blanks
  • Add 5-8 hashtags (mix local + category)
  • Share to Story with poll, question, or slider sticker
  • Reply to first 3-5 comments within 30 minutes (algorithm boost)
  • Engage with 5-10 other local accounts' posts (reciprocity)

90-Day Success Metrics

Track Weekly

  • ☐ Reels posted (target: 3)
  • ☐ Homes visited (target: 1-2)
  • ☐ Time spent editing (target: under 45 min)

Track Monthly

  • ☐ Total Reels posted (target: 12)
  • ☐ Average comments per Reel
  • ☐ DMs received from content
  • ☐ Profile visits trend

Do NOT Track (First 90 Days)

  • ✗ Follower count — vanity metric, lags engagement by months
  • ✗ Views per Reel — volatile, algorithm-dependent, discouraging early on
  • ✗ Comparison to larger accounts — different game entirely
